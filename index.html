    Improving Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Model (DDPM)

    One of our key findings regarding Diffusion Models is that while the model tends to follow a trajectory where isotropy increases, it does not explicitly ensure this condition. Therefore, the aim of this project is to enhance the training process of Diffusion Models by guiding the model to follow directions that actively promote greater isotropy in the generated samples.

    Visualization of the change in data distribution and the variation of the isotrpy in the forward and reverse processes

    Variation of isotropy with time steps in the forward process

    Using this approach, we improved the DDPM model and got some interesting results that show how well our changes work.
    In addition, we used Precision, Recall, Density, and Coverage, along with Frechet Inception Distance (FID) and Inception Score (IS), to measure the fidelity and diversity of the generated samples. These metrics helped us create more visualizations and gain better insights into the data distribution generation of both the original DDPM and our improved version. This highlights the importance of these new fidelity and diversity metrics for the research community and encourages further exploration of similar measures.
    The figures below illustrate how our improved algorithm enables the DDPM to better capture the structural details of the original data distribution and maintain the key semantics of the original distribution during the generation process.

    Central Banana 2D synthetic dataset: (a) True distribution points, colored based on the k = 5 nearest neighbor radius. (b) Points generated by DDPM, colored according to their true manifold span. (c) Points generated by Iso-Diffusion, colored according to their true manifold span.

    Scattered Moon 2D synthetic dataset: (a) True distribution points, colored based on the k = 5 nearest neighbor radius. (b) Points generated by DDPM, colored according to their true manifold span. (c) Points generated by Iso-Diffusion, colored according to their true manifold span.

    Swiss Roll 2D synthetic dataset: (a) True distribution points, colored based on the k = 5 nearest neighbor radius. (b) Points generated by DDPM, colored according to their true manifold span. (c) Points generated by Iso-Diffusion, colored according to their true manifold span.

    Multispectral Imaging for Condition Monitoring

    We have developed a Multispectral Imaging device including 13 spectral band spanning wavelengths from 360nm to 940nm.

    Cross section of the multispectral imaging device.

    The image capturing environment exhibited non-uniform intensity across the captured images, primarily due to misalignment between the reflective hemisphere and the monochrome camera in reflectance mode. To address this, a spatial correction was applied to ensure consistent intensity across the image plane. Furthermore, intensity variations were observed across different spectral bands, which were caused by inconsistencies in the illumination provided by the LEDs. To mitigate this issue, a spectral correction was performed, ensuring uniformity in the captured intensity across all spectral bands.

    Pixel intensity comparison among raw, spatially and spectrally corrected images.

    Currently, we are working on these algorithms to enhance the correction process and to make it robust.
